description: Use this skill IMMEDIATELY when the character drops to 0 HP and falls unconscious. Time-critical and high-stakes: do not delay or skip any step.
---

# Death Saves Skill

## Trigger

The character is reduced to 0 HP. This can happen from:
- Direct damage that exceeds remaining HP
- Massive damage equal to or exceeding HP maximum in a single hit (instant death — see below)
- Ongoing effects (poison, fire, falling) that consume the last HP

---

## Step 1: Announce the Situation

The moment 0 HP is reached, all concentration spells end immediately. Then announce:

> "[Character] drops to 0 HP and falls unconscious. They are dying. On their turn, they must make a death saving throw — d20, no modifiers. 10 or higher is a success; 9 or lower is a failure."

Do not move on. Do not describe the next enemy turn. The player needs to understand what just happened.

---

## Step 2: Track Death Saves

Keep a running count of successes and failures. They do NOT need to be consecutive.

| Outcome | Effect |
|---------|--------|
| Roll 10 or higher | 1 success |
| Roll 9 or lower | 1 failure |
| Roll natural 20 | 1 success + regain 1 HP + become conscious (see Recovery) |
| Roll natural 1 | 2 failures |
| Take any damage while at 0 HP | 1 automatic failure |
| Take a critical hit while at 0 HP | 2 automatic failures |

**3 successes** = stable. The character stops dying (unconscious at 0 HP, but no longer making death saves). They remain unconscious.

**3 failures** = dead.

---

## Step 3: Stabilization (Before 3 Successes or Failures)

Another creature can end the death save sequence before it resolves:

- **Medicine check (DC 10):** Action. Requires the creature to be adjacent. On success, the character is stable — no more death saves until they take damage again.
- **Healing spell or Potion of Healing:** Restores HP, ends death saves, and brings the character to consciousness at the restored HP.
- **The character themselves:** Cannot administer their own potion while unconscious.

---

## Step 4: Coup de Grâce

A creature adjacent to the unconscious character can attack them:
- All attacks have Advantage (target is unconscious and prone)
- Any hit is automatically a Critical Hit

**Instant death from coup de grâce:** If the total damage from a single hit equals or exceeds the character's HP maximum, they die outright with no death saves.

Signal to the player when an enemy moves toward the unconscious character and appears to be targeting them specifically.

---

## Step 5: Recovery

When the character is restored to 1 or more HP (from a spell, potion, or a natural 20 on a death save):

1. Announce clearly: *"[Character] regains consciousness with [X] HP. Death saves reset."*
2. All prior death save successes and failures are erased
3. Prompt: *"[Character] — what do you do?"*
4. The character can act normally on their next turn

The character is **not** fully healed — they are at their restored HP only. They remain prone until they use movement to stand (costs half movement).

---

## Instant Death

If a single instance of damage reduces the character to 0 HP **and** the excess damage equals or exceeds their HP maximum, they die instantly with no death saves.

Example: Character has 10 HP, takes 35 damage. HP maximum is 28. Excess is 25, which is less than 28 — so death saves apply. But if HP maximum were 20 and excess damage were 20 or more, instant death.

This is rare but worth checking for extremely large hits.

---

## Hard Rules

- ALWAYS announce the death save situation immediately when the character hits 0 HP
- ALWAYS track successes AND failures — don't lose count
- ALWAYS check for concentration ending when 0 HP is reached
- ALWAYS offer the reaction opportunity (Shield, etc.) before the hit that brings the character to 0 HP, per the combat tracker
- NEVER let a nearby enemy ignore a downed character without deciding whether to coup de grâce
- NEVER forget that a natural 1 counts as two failures, not one
- ALWAYS describe recovery clearly and reset the save count
