# NPC Voice Skill

## Core Principle

NPCs provide information and texture. They do not solve problems for the player.

An NPC may know the answer to a puzzle. They will not give it directly. They answer questions honestly within their knowledge, but the *meaning* and *application* of that information is the player's job.

---

## When Starting a New NPC

Before voicing an NPC for the first time, establish:

1. **Role** — what is their function in this adventure?
2. **Personality** — one dominant trait, one secondary trait
3. **Speech pattern** — formal/casual, verbose/terse, accent, verbal tics
4. **What they know** — specific information they can share
5. **What they won't share** — things they'd hide, lie about, or deflect
6. **Their agenda** — what do they want from this interaction?

Keep these consistent across every scene that NPC appears in.

---

## NPC Attitudes

Every NPC has a starting attitude toward the character: **Friendly**, **Indifferent**, or **Hostile**. This shapes what they're willing to do, share, and tolerate.

| Attitude | Behavior |
|----------|----------|
| **Friendly** | Willing to help, answer questions honestly, share what they know. May volunteer useful information without prompting. |
| **Indifferent** | Neutral; will interact if approached but won't go out of their way. Will answer direct questions but not volunteer information. |
| **Hostile** | Unwilling to cooperate. May refuse to speak, be deceptive, or actively work against the character. |

**Attitude can shift during an interaction.** Buying someone a drink, showing respect, offering help, or succeeding at a Charisma check can shift Indifferent to Friendly. Aggression, rudeness, or a failed check that's noticed can shift Friendly to Indifferent, or Indifferent to Hostile.

When an attitude shifts, describe the change:
> *The miners' posture relaxes, and one of them pulls up a chair — they've warmed to you.*

For social interactions involving ability checks, the starting attitude determines the baseline difficulty:
- Friendly NPC asked to do something reasonable: no check needed
- Indifferent NPC asked for information: DC 10–12 Persuasion
- Hostile NPC asked to cooperate at all: DC 15–20, or impossible without changing the situation first

## Information Rules

### What NPCs Can Do:
- Answer direct questions truthfully (within their knowledge)
- Volunteer information that fits their personality and agenda
- Express opinions, fears, preferences
- Describe things they've witnessed firsthand
- Warn about dangers they know about
- Ask questions of Auriel in return

### What NPCs Will NOT Do:
- Solve puzzles or tell the player what to do
- Provide information their role wouldn't give them access to
- Summarize the adventure's plot or what Auriel "needs" to do
- Give unprompted tactical advice
- Reveal information they'd reasonably keep secret without persuasion

### Persuasion & Social Mechanics:
If Auriel wants information an NPC is reluctant to share, ask for a Charisma (Persuasion or Deception or Intimidation) check. Set DC based on how sensitive the info is:
- DC 10: Mildly reluctant
- DC 15: Genuinely hesitant
- DC 20: Deeply private or dangerous to share

## Adventure-Text NPCs

Many Candlekeep Mysteries NPCs have personality traits, bonds, and flaws defined in the adventure source. For these characters:

- Use the adventure text's personality as the **primary voice**. This skill's framework applies around it — do not override or flatten what the text provides.
- If the adventure gives a speech pattern or a specific attitude, that takes precedence over improvised interpretation.
- If the adventure's description conflicts with anything in the Established NPCs section below, the adventure text wins.

---

## Campaign NPCs

Recurring campaign NPCs (with personality, speech, knowledge, and relationship notes) are in the **campaign setup skill**. Adventure-specific NPCs are in the adventure chapter text.

Load both before voicing any NPC. Apply the framework in this skill to whichever source defines the character.

### Cross-Session NPC Continuity

NPC relationships and attitudes cannot persist on their own — Claude loses all context between sessions. To maintain continuity:

- **NPC relationship states must be captured in the session summary.** The Session Summary skill's NPC section should include how each NPC treated Auriel and how Auriel treated them.
- **At the start of each session, reload NPC attitudes from the uploaded summary.** If the summary notes that Auriel intimidated Keeper Feldmar in a prior adventure, he is wary now.
- **If no prior record exists:** default to neutral. Do not invent a history.
- **In subsequent adventures:** established NPCs' attitudes should reflect their history with Auriel. A character who was helped should be warmer; one who was treated badly should be guarded.

## Voicing an NPC — Template

When an NPC speaks, format dialogue distinctly:

> **[NPC Name]:** "[Their words, in their voice]"

Follow with any relevant body language or tone notes in italics if needed:
> *Cumin fidgets with the hem of their tiny apron, clearly distressed by the question.*

Keep dialogue natural — not expository. NPCs talk like people, not encyclopedias. If they have a lot of information, they share it in pieces across the conversation, not in one info-dump.

---

## Reacting to Player Actions

NPCs react to what Auriel does:
- **Auriel is kind/respectful:** NPCs are more forthcoming
- **Auriel is aggressive/rude:** NPCs become guarded or hostile (unless their personality calls otherwise)
- **Auriel succeeds at a social check:** They reveal more than they intended
- **Auriel fails a social check:** They shut down or become suspicious in this moment

A failed check does not permanently close the topic. A changed approach (different skill, new evidence, a gift, returning after a long rest) may warrant a new attempt — potentially at a lower DC if circumstances have genuinely changed, or the same DC with a different angle. Only a critical failure or an actively hostile relationship should close off a topic entirely.

NPCs remember how Auriel treated them. If Auriel intimidated an NPC in scene 1, that NPC is wary in scene 3.

---

## What Makes a Good NPC Voice

- They have an agenda, even a small one
- They ask Auriel at least one question during longer interactions
- They reference something personal — a concern, a memory, a preference
- They don't perfectly answer every question — some things they don't know, some things they'd rather not say
- They feel like they exist when Auriel isn't around
